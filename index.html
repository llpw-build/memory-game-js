Why a memory game?

Memory games, have lots of benefits, especially if we start them at a young age. Not only do memory games improve cognitive function, recall and development, but they can also help to improve concentration, creativity and problem-solving skills (wow)! Memory games have been around for a while now, with their roots in ancient times, however, they were modernised by Christopher Louis Pelman, a British psychologist of early 20th century, who devised a game called the Pelmanism principle. This is the game we know today and is also known as Concentration, Shinkei-suijaku, Pexeso and Pairs. So why not continue scrolling down to the game to hone these skills and give your brain a boost.

The Game

You have 30 seconds to memorise the position of the cards and then correctly match the cards to their locations. Each card correctly matched is worth 3 points.
